FLIGHT PLANS

Inside the "iber_flightplans" folder
ebro.xml is the simplest one to use - an 11-waypoint plan of the major things to watch out for.

ebroMax.xml is a monster 164-waypoint detailed plan of the route.

ebroMax.gpx is a version in .gpx format which FG can read. It is the intermediary file and is the output of the LittleNavMap software used to plan this route.

ebroMax.pln is the 'source-code' of the LittleNavMap version.

TIMINGS

The full route and cutting out some bends
Takeoff will be from runway 25 of LERS (Reus) as soon after the meet-up time of 8pm BST as possible.

Following every single bend of both rivers, the distance is 540nm (from sea to shining sea alone) plus about 40nm for the airport runs. I have been just about able to do this at 300 KIAS with the SU-37, but it's not easy.

If the manoeuvrability of your particular operator-aircraft combination for the night won't allow this, you may need to cut a few corners. The ebroMax.xml plan is 463nm and ebro.xml is 348nm. The straight line distance between start and finish is just over 260nm. Choose wisely!

I will probably pootle along at about 250 KIAS following the route in general, so people can follow me more easily - whilst anyone wanting to play the boy-racer can go faster and leave no bend unflown.

It would be nice to do the full length of both rivers but, being realistic, an 8-pm departure is unlikely. It's unfortunate that LERS is so far from the Ebro estuary. The most likely start to the night will therefore involve going directly to WP6:



This will give us an extra 15 minutes or so.
